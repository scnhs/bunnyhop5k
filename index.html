About

The Bunny Hop 5K is an annual race that raises funds and awareness for charity. The run is hosted by the State College Area High School National Honor Society.

All proceeds from the first annual Bunny Hop 5K will directly support the Four Diamonds Fund in support of Maryann Newcomb, a State High student and NHS member who is bravely battling leukemia. The Four Diamonds Fund works to conquer childhood cancer by covering the expenses of families and supporting innovative, life-saving research.

Maryann Newcomb

"Growing up in State College I was introduced to Four Diamonds at a young age, I knew it was a good organization but I never knew how impactful it could be. Being on the receiving end of Four Diamonds has completely changed my view of the organization. Having leukemia is already stressful enough as is. Fortunately because of Four Diamonds other stress causing factors such as paying for treatment, which is astronomical, is taken care of. In addition, I am fortunate to have such a good protocol and survival rate because of the research that Four Diamonds does. Four Diamonds makes such a huge impact on the lives and families of kids battling cancer, I wouldnâ€™t be where I am today without them."

Four Diamonds Fund

The Four Diamonds Fund is a charitable organization based out of the Penn State Milton S. Hershey Medical Center. Its ultimate objective is to "conquer childhood cancer".

Additional Information

The Bunny Hop 5K will be on Saturday, April 8th at Circleville Park. Race start time will be at 9am, with day of registration beginning at 8am.

Both runners and walkers are encouraged to participate, as well as teams. Costumes are allowed and encouraged for this fun event in support of childhood cancer patients. On race day, prizes will be given to the best-dressed team, fastest runners, the team which raises the most money, and more! Food and beverages will be provided.
